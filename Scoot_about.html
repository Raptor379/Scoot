FAQs
How it works
How do I download the app?
To download the Scoot app, you can search “Scoot” in both the App and Google Play stores. An even simpler way to do it would be to click the relevant link at the bottom of this page and you’ll be re-directed to the correct page.
Can I find a nearby Scoots?
To find a nearby Scoot, simply open the Scoot app on your smartphone. The app will display a map showing the location of all available scooters around you. You can easily locate the closest one and start your ride in just a few taps!
Do I need a license to ride?
In most areas, you don’t need a specific license to ride a Scoot. However, it's important to check local regulations, as some cities may require riders to be of a certain age or have a valid ID. Always ensure you follow the guidelines for a safe ride.
How it works
Should I wear a helmet?
Yes, please do! All cities have different laws. But we strongly strongly strongly recommend always wearing a helmet regardless of the local laws. We like you and we want you to be as safe as possible while Scooting.
How about the rules & regulations?
Riding Scoots comes with certain rules to ensure the safety of all users. Always obey traffic signals, ride in bike lanes where available, and park responsibly. Make sure to familiarize yourself with local laws regarding electric scooters to avoid fines or penalties.
What if I damage my Scoot?
If you accidentally damage your Scoot during your ride, it’s essential to report the incident via the app immediately. Depending on the severity of the damage, you may be held responsible for repair costs. Always treat the scooter with care to avoid any issues.
